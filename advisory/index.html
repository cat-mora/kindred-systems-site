Advisory & Consulting

Technical capability and business direction are different things.

Many organisations have people who understand the tools and people who understand the business. What is often missing is someone who can sit between the two: translating commercial priorities into clear implementation decisions, and keeping both sides moving in the same direction.

That is what this advisory work is.

What this is

AI strategy and implementation advisory.

This is not AI education or generic strategy consulting. It is advisory work for businesses that need someone to sit between the commercial and technical sides and help both move in the same direction. Where a contained AI solution is the right answer, Kindred Systems can design and build that directly.

What the work involves

Working alongside your leadership, operational and technical teams to clarify where AI can create real value, what should happen first, and how to ensure what gets built is actually adopted.

  • Prioritising AI opportunities and defining the strongest first use case
  • Translating business problems into clear implementation priorities
  • Aligning leadership, operations and technical teams around a shared direction
  • Reviewing proposed AI initiatives before significant budget is committed
  • Identifying workflow, data and adoption issues before they become expensive problems
  • Guiding implementation decisions as the work develops
  • Facilitating adoption workshops tied to a specific rollout: helping staff understand how a new AI system fits their work, where human judgment stays essential, and how to handle edge cases

What it is not

Being clear about scope protects you from a poor engagement as much as it protects the work.

  • This is not enterprise-scale software development, whole-of-business systems integration, or outsourced CIO or technical delivery. If you need that, a specialist technical firm is the right fit.
  • This is not generic AI training. Adoption workshops are scoped within a specific rollout, not delivered as standalone sessions on how to use AI tools generally.
  • Legal, compliance, security and privacy advice should sit with appropriately qualified specialists. I can help you think through the right questions to ask and what to look for, but I am not a licensed adviser in those areas.
  • Depending on what the advisory work reveals, the right delivery path may be your internal team, a specialist technical partner, or a Kindred-managed AI employee built and maintained by us. The advisory work helps identify which.

AI Clarity Audit

  • Written AI opportunity roadmap for the defined scope
  • Recommended first initiative with rationale
  • Indicative effort, cost and risk for the top opportunities

02

AI Strategy Day

A$3,500 + GST

A single focused advisory session for businesses that have a specific question, decision or stakeholder alignment challenge to work through. This is not an open-ended discovery day.

A defined outcome or question is agreed in writing before the day begins. The session is designed to resolve that question, not to surface new ones. If the day reveals scope that cannot be resolved in one session, next steps are scoped and agreed before anyone leaves.

Strategy Days are advisory sessions. They are designed to resolve a decision, align stakeholders or clarify a next phase. They are not build, configuration or integration sessions.

This works well for

  • Leadership workshops to align on AI strategy and priorities
  • Vendor or platform evaluation with an independent commercial lens
  • Build-versus-buy or tool selection decisions
  • Stress-testing an existing AI roadmap before presenting it to a board
  • Briefing an internal team before they begin implementation
Booking requirement A defined outcome or question must be agreed before the day is booked. If you are not yet sure what the question is, the AI Clarity Audit is the right starting point.
